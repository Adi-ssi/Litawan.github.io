Tips for Cooking Like a Pro

  • Learn to chop properly. Use the right knife for the task and practice consistent cuts for even cooking.
  • Understand fundamental cooking methods like sautéing, roasting, boiling, and steaming.
  • Always read the recipe from start to finish before you start cooking.
  • Double-check ingredient measurements and steps to avoid surprises.
  • Prepare and measure all your ingredients before you start cooking. It makes the process smoother and prevents mistakes.
  • Use fresh herbs, vegetables, and proteins to enhance flavor and texture.
  • Store spices in a cool, dry place to retain their potency.
  • Sample your dish at every stage to balance seasoning. Don't be afraid to adjust salt, pepper, or spices.

Sustainable/Zero Waste Cooking Tips

  • 1. Use Bones to Make Stock
    After cooking meat, especially chicken, beef, or pork, save the bones. You can use them to make homemade stock. Simply simmer the bones with water, vegetable scraps, and seasonings like garlic, onion, herbs, and salt for a few hours. This will create a flavorful base for soups, stews, and sauces.
  • 2. Repurpose Leftover Vegetables
    Instead of throwing away leftover vegetables or scraps, like carrot tops, celery leaves, or onion peels, save them to make a vegetable stock. Just like with bones, simmer the scraps with water and seasonings to make a rich, flavorful stock.
  • 3. Save Citrus Peels
    Don’t toss out citrus peels! They can be used in many ways:
    • Zest the peel for flavoring in desserts, dressings, or marinades.
    • Infuse the peel in water or vinegar to create a natural cleaner.
    • Candy the peels or add them to homemade marmalades.
  • 4. Use Stale Bread for Croutons or Bread Pudding
    Instead of throwing away stale bread, turn it into homemade croutons for salads or soups. Simply cube the bread, toss it with olive oil, garlic, and seasoning, and bake until crispy. Alternatively, use stale bread for bread pudding, a great way to use up both old bread and leftover milk or eggs.
  • 5. Turn Scraps into Chips or Fries
    Leftover potato or vegetable scraps can be turned into homemade chips or fries. Simply slice them thinly, toss with olive oil and seasoning, and bake until crispy. This works with potato peels, carrot ends, and even zucchini or sweet potato peels.
  • 6. Make Smoothies with Overripe Fruit
    Overripe fruit like bananas, berries, or peaches can be frozen and used in smoothies. You can also blend them into a puree for desserts, muffins, or pancake toppings.
  • 7. Use Coffee Grounds for Flavor
    Used coffee grounds can add a rich, earthy flavor to chocolate-based desserts or even be used to season meats before grilling. They also work well in composting or as a natural exfoliant in DIY body scrubs.
  • 8. Reuse Eggshells in the Garden
    Instead of throwing away eggshells, crush them and add them to your garden. They provide calcium to the soil and can help deter pests like slugs and snails.
  • 9. Freeze Leftovers for Later Use
    Don’t let leftovers go to waste—store them in reusable containers or freezer bags for later meals. This works for soups, sauces, rice, pasta, and even small portions of cooked vegetables.
  • 10. Use Herb Stems for Flavoring
    Instead of discarding the stems of fresh herbs like cilantro, parsley, or basil, use them to infuse sauces, stocks, or stews. You can also blend them into pesto or grind them with spices for a homemade seasoning mix.
  • 11. Repurpose Coffee or Tea Bags
    Used tea bags can be added to your homemade stock, especially if you’re making a vegetable stock. You can also repurpose old coffee grounds for making natural scrub or adding to compost.
  • 12. Save Veggie Scraps for Future Cooking
    Keep a bag or container in your freezer for veggie scraps like onion skins, carrot tops, or tomato cores. You can use these scraps later to enhance the flavor of stocks, soups, or sauces.

By being mindful of your food scraps and leftovers, you can make your cooking more sustainable, save money, and minimize food waste in your kitchen!
