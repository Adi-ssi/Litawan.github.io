Mac and Cheese Recipe

Ingredients

  • Macaroni pasta - 2 cups (uncooked)
  • Butter - 4 tbsp
  • All-purpose flour - 2 tbsp
  • Milk - 2 cups (preferably whole milk)
  • Shredded cheddar cheese - 2 cups (you can mix sharp and mild for flavor depth)
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese - 1 cup
  • Salt - to taste
  • Ground black pepper - to taste
  • Garlic powder - 1/2 tsp (optional)
  • Paprika - 1/2 tsp (optional for extra flavor and color)
  • Breadcrumbs - 1/4 cup (optional, for topping)

Instructions

  1. Cook 2 cups of macaroni pasta in a large pot of boiling salted water according to the package instructions, typically about 7-9 minutes. Once cooked, drain the pasta and set it aside.
  2. In the same pot, melt 4 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Once melted, sprinkle in 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour and whisk continuously for 1-2 minutes to create a smooth paste (roux).
  3. Slowly pour in 2 cups of milk while whisking to avoid lumps. Continue stirring until the sauce thickens, about 4-5 minutes.
  4. Once the sauce has thickened, reduce the heat and gradually stir in 2 cups of shredded cheddar cheese and 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese until fully melted and the sauce is creamy.
  5. Season the sauce with salt, black pepper, garlic powder, and paprika to taste.
  6. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir to coat evenly.
  7. If you prefer a crispy topping, preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Pour the mac and cheese into a greased baking dish, and sprinkle 1/4 cup of breadcrumbs on top. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly.
  8. Once baked, remove from the oven and let it cool for a couple of minutes before serving. Enjoy!
